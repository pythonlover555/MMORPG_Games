# MMORPG_Games


1. Rangers of Oblivion
  ◈ [Video]
  https://drive.google.com/file/d/1TUSRgvphX6uPo00wrrJ10NQywj0O9uJH/view?usp=sharing
  
  ◈ [Overview & Asset Profile]
  ▷ Service Period: Circa 2018 ~ 2021
  ▷ Description: Rangers of Oblivion is an asset developed by the Chinese giant NetEase, a project that successfully pioneered the console-like "Monster Hunter" genre in the mobile space. In a market saturated with auto-combat, it implemented 100% manual, non-targeting action and achieved high technical and aesthetic accomplishment by applying sophisticated cartoon-rendering textures to realistically proportioned character models. Although the service has been terminated, its source code holds immense technical value for any developer aiming to produce a high-quality action MMO.
  
  ◈ [Core Gameplay & Differentiation]
  ▷ Description: This game's experience focuses on the "joy of the process, not just the result." The player's skill determines the success of a hunt, and the "part-breaking" system, which requires destroying specific monster parts (tails, horns) to obtain rare materials, adds strategic depth to the farming process. Furthermore, its classless system, where roles and skills change completely based on the equipped weapon (greatsword, bow, lance), maximizes replay value by allowing players to experience diverse playstyles. This is a key differentiator that motivates players to improve their skills rather than just compete on stats.
  
  ◈ [Revenue Generation & Core Driver]
  ▷ Core Revenue Driver: The Gacha is designed as a "mandatory entry barrier" to access endgame content, creating a statistical wall that skill alone cannot overcome, thus compelling purchases.
  ▷ Description: The revenue model of Rangers of Oblivion is engineered to perfectly align with its hardcore gameplay. Instead of simple stat boosts, it sells "Souls" via a Gacha system, which grant unique active/passive skills. This is perceived not as an option but as an essential purchase for tackling high-level content, as it broadens a player's strategic options. Additionally, it targets heavy users by limiting the number of daily attempts on top-tier monsters and selling tickets for extra entries. This is a clever mechanism that controls the in-game economy and generates consistent revenue.
  
  ◈ [Strategic Value & Acquisition Proposal]
  ▷ Description: The core value of this asset lies in the "top-tier mobile 3D non-targeting action engine" itself. The technology that stably synchronizes the complex physics interactions between multiple players and giant monsters in a mobile environment is the culmination of years of development expertise. Therefore, this game provides an ideal technical blueprint for a developer targeting the hardcore action market. Especially when aiming for core gamers in North America and Europe, its source code would be a powerful foundation to dramatically shorten development time and meet the high standards of the market.

2. Dawn of Isles
  ◈ [Video]
  https://drive.google.com/file/d/1a04br5XAv1lWxb19QPeCwLDUHuNaIMUA/view?usp=sharing
  
  ◈ [Overview & Asset Profile]
  ▷ Service Period: Circa 2019 ~ 2021
  ▷ Description: Dawn of Isles is an original project that blends the successful elements of "The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild" and "Animal Crossing" into the MMORPG genre. Moving beyond simple combat and progression, sandbox elements like decorating a personal island, gathering resources, and interacting with other players constitute half of the game. Rendered in a bright and warm toon-shading style without violent or sexualized content, it is an asset with the potential to appeal to a wide user base, regardless of gender or age.
  
  ◈ [Core Gameplay & Differentiation]
  ▷ Description: This game offers players a comprehensive virtual world experience of "exploring, creating, and interacting." A major feature is the "elemental interaction system," where elements like fire, water, and wind interact to create various strategic outcomes. For example, using a fire skill on an oil puddle creates a powerful flame zone. Outside of combat, the process of chopping wood and mining ore to build homes and tools is core content in itself, granting players high levels of freedom and achievement, which forms a strong attachment to the game.
  
  ◈ [Revenue Generation & Core Driver]
  ▷ Core Revenue Driver: It establishes a stable, dual-revenue structure by applying the "Pet = Power" formula to combat players and the "Time = Money" formula to life-skill players, monetizing both groups effectively.
  ▷ Description: The revenue model is meticulously designed to target both combat and life-skill players. Pets, which account for a significant portion of combat power, are obtained via Gacha, and their evolution structure, requiring multiple copies of the same pet, induces repeat purchases. Furthermore, "speed-up" tickets that instantly complete long construction or crafting times are a classic yet effective revenue source for impatient players. Finally, premium decorative items for the island or cosmetic gifts for friends encourage spending not for competition, but for self-expression and relationship building.
  
  ◈ [Strategic Value & Acquisition Proposal]
  ▷ Description: This asset holds the potential to pioneer a unique market for "lifestyle sandbox MMOs." Technically, its core assets are the server architecture that organically connects individual player islands (personalized spaces) with the public world and the elemental interaction logic. For a developer planning a next-generation community game with enhanced metaverse and social features, rather than a combat-focused MMO, this game's structure serves as a perfect reference. It can exhibit strong competitiveness, especially in markets targeting female and younger demographics.

3. Era of Legends
  ◈ [Video]
  https://drive.google.com/file/d/1xwI9VdoDx_iVDWsQCyB4nckzlZ4sTkuG/view?usp=sharing
  
  ◈ [Overview & Asset Profile]
  ▷ Service Period: Circa 2019 ~ Present (in sunset/shutdown phase)
  ▷ Description: Era of Legends is one of the most faithful mobile adaptations of the classic PC MMORPG formula, epitomized by "World of Warcraft." It focuses on delivering an experience familiar to PC MMO players, featuring vast open worlds, clear role divisions (Tank/DPS/Healer), and large-scale raid content. The graphics adopt a bright, heroic cartoon style favored in the North American market, making it an asset optimized for targeting a stable and proven market.
  
  ◈ [Core Gameplay & Differentiation]
  ▷ Description: The core of this game is "familiarity and stability." Players can immediately enjoy the fun of cooperative play they remember from PC MMOs without needing to learn complex new systems. A key differentiator is the "Transformation System," which allows players to turn the tide of battle by transforming into powerful beasts or mythical beings. This adds an unpredictable variable to otherwise standard combat and provides strategic depth, especially as an "ultimate skill" in PvP or boss fights.
  
  ◈ [Revenue Generation & Core Driver]
  ▷ Core Revenue Driver: It employs a clear "Spend = Rank" VIP system that intentionally creates disparity and a sense of deprivation among players, stimulating competitive psychology to drive continuous spending.
  ▷ Description: The revenue model follows an aggressive and direct Pay-to-Win (P2W) structure, which is highly effective for securing high revenue in a short period. The VIP level increases based on cumulative spending, granting absolute advantages in growth, such as extra dungeon entries, XP bonuses, and access to exclusive shops. This is a powerful mechanism that intentionally creates a gap between players to stimulate competition and drive spending. Furthermore, it sells mounts and pets that directly impact combat power, not just for transportation, via limited-time Gacha, stimulating both collection and power-progression desires.
  
  ◈ [Strategic Value & Acquisition Proposal]
  ▷ Description: The value of this asset lies less in innovation and more in being a "blueprint for a proven revenue model." It is a highly attractive option for a developer looking to quickly launch a standard, high-revenue MMO with minimal risk. Every monetization mechanic in this game—from the VIP system to package deals and event design—has been proven effective across countless titles. Therefore, even without a deep understanding of the market, a developer could expect stable commercial success simply by "reskinning" this game's source code with new graphic assets.

4. Lumia Saga
  ◈ [Video]
  https://drive.google.com/file/d/1Jz3BK5IV5Csu8X9K-60ilAZPuTl7E-2I/view?usp=sharing
  
  ◈ [Overview & Asset Profile]
  ▷ Service Period: Circa 2019 ~ 2021
  ▷ Description: Lumia Saga established a unique visual identity by combining cute SD (Super-Deformed) characters with high-quality shaders that realistically render the texture of metal and leather on equipment. Beneath its charming exterior, it hides a deeply complex equipment customization system and is an asset designed to target players who value social and community activities.
  
  ◈ [Core Gameplay & Differentiation]
  ▷ Description: The game's most original system is the "Magic Core." Instead of acquiring finished weapons, players collect and combine hundreds of "Cores" that determine a weapon's appearance and stats. By combining different cores for the "blade," "hilt," and "center," players can create a one-of-a-kind weapon. This is a highly efficient and creative system that enables thousands of item combinations with limited development resources, providing players with endless fun in farming and collecting.
  
  ◈ [Revenue Generation & Core Driver]
  ▷ Core Revenue Driver: By selling "parts" (Magic Cores) instead of finished products via Gacha, it creates endless combinations and minute performance differences, infinitely stimulating the collector's desire in players who seek the perfect setup.
  ▷ Description: The revenue model is cleverly built around this "Magic Core" system. Players spend on Gacha to obtain cores with the best stats and rarest appearances. By selling "parts" instead of the weapon itself, players are continuously motivated to spend in pursuit of a better combination. Additionally, it generates strong social-based revenue by selling items for in-game weddings, such as rings and couple-exclusive outfits, which cater to players' desires to show off and commemorate their relationships.
  
  ◈ [Strategic Value & Acquisition Proposal]
  ▷ Description: The core value of this asset lies in the technically superb "source code of the modular equipment customization system (Magic Core system)." Transplanting this system into another Unity project could drastically reduce 3D modeling costs while offering users a near-infinite level of item diversity. For a developer targeting the casual and social game market, especially one planning a project with strong avatar customization and housing systems, this game's source code would be an incredibly valuable technical asset.

5. Aura Kingdom 2
  ◈ [Video]
  https://drive.google.com/file/d/1qKasBFwUdt212WsFazSiIJuzhf5j0xZp/view?usp=sharing
  
  ◈ [Overview & Asset Profile]
  ▷ Service Period: Circa 2020 ~ Present (in sunset/shutdown phase)
  ▷ Description: Aura Kingdom 2 is a project that successfully ported a famous PC game IP to mobile, boasting high-quality Japanese anime-style 3D graphics and flashy action sequences. The core of the game is the "Guardian (Eidolon)" system, where players fight alongside spiritual partners. This is a hybrid model that perfectly integrates the fun of a "collection-style RPG" within the framework of a traditional MMORPG.
  
  ◈ [Core Gameplay & Differentiation]
  ▷ Description: The gameplay experience is focused on "bonds and cooperative combat with a partner." Eidolons are not mere pets; they are companions with their own personalities and stories who execute powerful combo skills with the player. The player's combat style changes significantly depending on which Eidolon they partner with, providing both collection and strategic fun. The combat is fast-paced and stylish, filled with the spectacular skill effects favored by JRPG fans.
  
  ◈ [Revenue Generation & Core Driver]
  ▷ Core Revenue Driver: It directly monetizes fan attachment to attractive characters through a "Limit Break" system that requires multiple copies to perfect a single character, extracting massive repeat spending from loyal users.
  ▷ Description: This game's revenue model follows the most powerful formula of collection-style RPGs, serving as a catalyst for explosive revenue within the MMORPG genre. The core BM, accounting for the majority of revenue, is the Eidolon Gacha. Players spend to pull for attractive Eidolons, and a "Limit Break" (Transcend) system is in place, requiring multiple copies of the same character to unlock their full potential. This induces enormous repeat spending from users who grow attached to specific characters. Additionally, it sells costume sets that provide direct combat stats, not just cosmetic changes, motivating all players to purchase.
  
  ◈ [Strategic Value & Acquisition Proposal]
  ▷ Description: The value of this asset lies in its "hybrid revenue model that combines the long-term retention of an MMORPG with the short-term explosive revenue of a collection-style RPG." This system's effectiveness has been thoroughly proven in the market. Therefore, for a company that owns a strong character IP (from a webtoon, anime, etc.) and wishes to enter the MMORPG market, the source code of Aura Kingdom 2 provides a verified framework to monetize that IP in the fastest and most effective way. This would be an optimal strategy to minimize the immense risks of new development and maximize the probability of success.
